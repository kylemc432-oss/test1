Beginner tips

  • Pace (running): run slow — slower than feels natural. You should be able to hold a conversation. Speed comes later; consistency comes first.
  • Form (lifting): on your heavier compounds (squat, deadlift, bench), prioritize form over the number on the bar, especially in week 1–2 while you're finding your working weights.
  • Progressive overload: if you hit the top of an exercise's rep range on every set, add a small amount of weight (2.5–5%) next time you do that lift. If you miss the bottom of the range, hold the weight and try again.
  • Recovery: this is a demanding 7-day week (running + lifting). Watch for nagging joint pain, poor sleep, or dragging energy — those are signs to dial back intensity on a day rather than push through.
  • Shoes & gear: a proper pair of running shoes meaningfully cuts injury risk. For lifting, a stable flat shoe (or lifting shoe) beats running shoes under a barbell.
  • Missed a day? just pick the plan back up on your next scheduled day. Don't try to "double up" to catch up.
  • Before you start: if you're new to structured exercise or have any health concerns, it's worth checking with a doctor before beginning a program like this.
